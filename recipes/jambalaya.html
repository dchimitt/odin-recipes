Cajun Jambalaya

Recipe taken from The Spruce Eats

Description:

This is a recipe for making spicy cajun jambalaya.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless/skinless chicken breast, cubed
  • 4 tsp Cajun seasoning with salt
  • 2 tsp neutral oil (canola)
  • 12 ounces andouille or smoked sausage, cut into coins
  • 1 cup diced yellow onion
  • 1 cup diced green bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced celery
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice, rinsed until water drains clear
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 cups chicken broth OR 3 cubes chicken bouillion dissolved in 3 cups water
  • 4 medium scallions, thinly sliced

Steps:

  1. In medium bowl, combine chicken with 2 tsp cajun seasoning and toss
  2. Heat oil in pot on medium heat. Add chicken and brown all sides, 8-10 minutes. Set aside.
  3. Add sausage to pot and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Transfer to chicken.
  4. Add onion, bell pepper, and celery to the pot. Saute over medium heat until tender and translucent, 5-6 minutes.
  5. Add garlic, rice, Worcestershire, bay leaves, thyme, black pepper, chicken brother, and 2 tsp cajun. Add chicken and sausage back to pot, stir. Bring to boil on high heat.
  6. Reduce heat to low and simmer while covered until rice is fully cooked and fluffy, about 30 minutes. If rice is still wet, cook uncovered another 5-10 minutes.
  7. Serve garnished with scallions.
